Creative Writing Challenge

A Folk Tale with a Twist

"There are two sides to every story..."

The Mission

History is written by the victors, but today, you will write for the forgotten. Choose a famous folk tale (like The Raven, Cinderella, or Red Riding Hood) and retell it from the perspective of the Villain or a Minor Character.

What is "Misunderstood"?

A misunderstood character isn't necessarily "good," but they have reasons for their actions that the hero ignores.

Medusa (Greek Myth)

Monster? Or a victim cursed by jealous gods, forced to live in isolation?

Captain Hook (Peter Pan)

Villain? Or an old pirate tormented by a flying boy who cut off his hand?

The Wicked Witch (Oz)

Evil tyrant? Or a grieving sister trying to protect her family's heirloom (the shoes)?

Your Writing Steps:

  • 1 Select a Character: Pick someone misunderstood. (e.g., The Chief who lost the sun, the Wolf who just wanted lunch).
  • 2 Plan the Timeline: What happened before the main event? What was happening when the "hero" arrived?
  • 3 Draft the Narrative: Write 150-200 words using the Grammar Toolkit below.
Example: The Chief from "The Raven"

"I had learned that the box contained the sun, so I guarded it carefully. One day, while my grandson was playing, he began crying for the box. I didn't know he had turned himself into a bird and was planning to steal it!"

Grammar in Action

Narrative Tenses

Past Simple

The Main Events

Use for completed actions that move the story forward.
"He stole the box."

Past Continuous

The Background / Interruptions

Use for actions in progress when something else happened.
"While I was guarding it..."

Past Perfect

The Backstory

Use for actions that happened before the main story started.
"I had warned him before..."

Storyteller's Tip

To make the twist effective, justify the "villain's" actions. Maybe they were just trying to protect something (like the Chief) or they were simply hungry!
